Why this assessment matters

The UK Government's AI Skills Competency Framework (DSIT/Skills England, 2025) identifies the exact competencies businesses need to adopt AI successfully — yet most organisations are flying blind.

1 in 6 UK businesses currently using AI (mid-2025)
21% of UK workers feel confident using AI at work
45% less likely: SMEs vs large firms to adopt AI

This 20-question diagnostic maps your organisation against the framework's five domains: technical skills, responsible AI, non-technical competencies, change management readiness, and data governance. Takes 4–6 minutes.

Domain 1 of 5
Technical AI Skills
Practical competencies to operate, monitor and guide AI systems — from prompt writing to workflow automation (Framework: Technical domain, all job levels)
Question 1 of 20
Can staff across your organisation write effective, structured prompts to generate useful outputs from AI tools?
Framework ref: "Write prompts for AI tools" — entry-level technical skill required at all levels
Question 2 of 20
Are AI tools actively integrated into day-to-day workflows (scheduling, reporting, summarisation, customer comms)?
Framework ref: "Apply AI tools in workflows" and "Set AI tools for task automation" — operational integration
Question 3 of 20
Can your team use AI-powered analytics or dashboard tools to generate data insights without specialist data science expertise?
Framework ref: "Analyse data using AI tools" — supports evidence-based decisions without specialist skills
Question 4 of 20
Does your business use low-code or no-code AI automation platforms to streamline repetitive processes?
Framework ref: "Use low-code automation tools" — enables efficiency without coding knowledge

Domain 2 of 5
Responsible & Ethical AI
Upholding ethical principles, GDPR compliance, bias detection, and governance accountability (Framework: Responsible & Ethical domain)
Question 5 of 20
Is there a named executive or senior leader accountable for AI ethics and governance in your organisation?
Framework ref: "Who at the executive level is responsible?" — governance accountability gap identified by AI policy advisors
Question 6 of 20
Do your staff know how to identify and flag potential bias in AI-generated outputs (e.g. in recruitment, customer decisions, content)?
Framework ref: "Identify bias in AI outputs" — prevents harm in decisions affecting individuals and communities
Question 7 of 20
Does your organisation have documented policies on GDPR compliance specifically in relation to AI systems handling personal data?
Framework ref: "Apply data protection principles" — GDPR compliance is legal and reputational risk management
Question 8 of 20
Are staff empowered and trained to intervene or escalate when AI outputs seem incorrect, harmful, or inappropriate?
Framework ref: "Guide responses to inappropriate AI decisions" — prevents over-reliance on AI in sensitive contexts

Domain 3 of 5
Non-Technical AI Skills & Leadership
Critical thinking, AI literacy, strategy planning, peer learning and executive modelling of AI behaviours (Framework: Non-technical domain — most urgently needed)
Question 9 of 20
Can your senior leaders articulate a clear AI strategy — including goals, responsible use expectations, and success metrics?
Framework ref: "Plan AI strategy and responsible use expectations" — managerial-level non-technical skill
Question 10 of 20
Do your leaders personally use and model AI tools — demonstrating effective, ethical use to their teams?
Framework ref: Leaders "should model effective and ethical use for others" — executive behaviour shapes organisational culture
Question 11 of 20
Does your organisation have a culture of peer learning around AI — where staff share discoveries, tools, and best practices informally?
Framework ref: "Adapt to new AI tools and contribute to peer learning" — builds team competencies inclusively
Question 12 of 20
How well does your organisation critically evaluate AI tool outputs rather than accepting them at face value?
Framework ref: "Critical thinking, subjective reasoning" — cited as most urgently needed and least addressed

Question 14 of 20

Question 15 of 20
Is AI upskilling built into your talent development and HR strategy — not just as one-off training events?
Framework ref: "AI upskilling is not confined to technical specialists — it is distributed across all organisational roles"
Question 16 of 20
Does your business have a dedicated budget or investment plan for AI tools, infrastructure, and skills development?
Framework ref: Managerial-level strategic competency — "decisions that shape how AI is adopted including policy, investment, and governance"
